Emil is a young boy who wears a white hooded robe and a mask to hide his face. He is initially encountered by the game's main protagonists, androids 2B and 9S, while wandering alone in a desolate area of the game world. Emil is revealed to be a genetically engineered human with powerful magical abilities, including the ability to petrify enemies with his gaze.

Emil is a tragic figure, having been forced to become a living weapon by a shadowy organization known as the Project Gestalt. He is the only survivor of the project, which was intended to create superhuman soldiers to combat a deadly alien invasion. Emil's incredible power also comes with a terrible price: his body is slowly deteriorating, and his mind is haunted by the guilt of the atrocities he was forced to commit.

Despite his tragic past and physical limitations, Emil is a loyal ally to 2B and 9S, helping them in their quest to defeat the alien invaders and uncover the truth behind the world they inhabit. Emil's role in the story becomes increasingly important as the game progresses, and his ultimate fate is one of the game's most poignant moments.

Emil Heads are small, robotic versions of Emil's head that can be found hidden throughout the game's world. They are often tucked away in hard-to-reach locations or hidden behind obstacles, and can be easy to miss if players are not paying close attention.

Collecting Emil Heads is not necessary to complete the game, but it does unlock a special side quest that explores Emil's backstory in more detail. As players collect more Emil Heads, they will unlock new entries in a special in-game encyclopedia that sheds light on Emil's past and his connection to the game's larger narrative.

While Emil Heads may seem like a lighthearted collectible at first glance, they actually play an important role in the game's story. As players progress through the game, they will learn more about Emil's tragic past and the terrible experiments that were performed on him. The Emil Heads serve as a reminder of Emil's humanity and his struggles, and collecting them can be a way for players to show their support for the character.
